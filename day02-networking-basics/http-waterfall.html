HTTP/1.1 vs HTTP/2: The Waterfall Race

A browser loading a page needs to fetch dozens of small files. HTTP/1.1 queues them across a handful of parallel connections. HTTP/2 multiplexes all of them over a single connection. Watch what that difference actually costs.

Page & network

Assumes a 150ms one-time connection setup (TCP + TLS), and — for HTTP/2 only — a small 2ms per-stream framing cost. HTTP/1.1's connection limit is real (browsers cap it per domain); HTTP/2 needs only one connection regardless of request count.
